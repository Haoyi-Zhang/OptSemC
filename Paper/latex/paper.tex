\begin{abstract}
Analytical SQL engines often describe optimizer behavior with public labels such as \texttt{pushdown}, \texttt{join}, or \texttt{cost based}.  Those labels are convenient, but they are too coarse to serve as equality predicates: a single \texttt{pushdown} cell can hide local rewrite, connector delegation, service-side stages, or runtime filtering.  A performance study can therefore compare different query-processing phenomena before any latency or plan-quality metric is interpreted.  OptSem-C makes this denominator explicit.  It records each public optimizer claim as a guarded contract over query features, operator/action kind, optimizer layer, execution placement, decision time, observability, and evidence provenance; then it asks which distinctions a comparison vocabulary preserves and which repaired fields are needed before the vocabulary can be trusted.  The schema, projection portfolio, source manifest, and repair-field universe are fixed before witness counting, and repairs are checked by source removal, probe-fold stability, engine-family stress, SQL executability, and visible-plan sanity checks.  On seven analytical SQL engines, 287 source-linked rules, and 4,216 executable probes, keyword, operator-only, and yes/no vocabularies create 254, 238, and 6 source-supported false equivalences.  The 498 headline projection-witness records cover 486 distinct probes; they are denominator errors, not private optimizer bug claims.  The missing distinctions are small and query-processing specific: placement repairs keyword collisions, optimizer layer repairs operator-only collisions, and layer plus placement separates all headline records within the admitted corpus.  OptSem-C turns coarse optimizer feature labels into replayable contract-denominator claims with public evidence, finite counter-witnesses, repair certificates, and a rerunnable finite-audit path.
\end{abstract}
