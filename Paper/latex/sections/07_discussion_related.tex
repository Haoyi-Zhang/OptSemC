\section{Discussion and Limitations}
\label{sec:discussion}

\textbf{Scope and protocol.}  Public optimizer claims should name action, guard, layer, placement, decision time, and evidence surface; otherwise adapters, remote sources, and service stages collapse into one phenomenon.  Before ranking latency, cardinality error, or plan quality, a study should test the shared public contract relation and then retain separating fields, narrow the claim, or report mixed phenomena.  JOB, TPC-H, and TPC-DS standardize inputs and metrics, not contract equality; our counts remain lower bounds over public evidence.

\textbf{Boundary of the claim.}  OptSem-C does not infer private cost models, rank workload performance, or turn a finite public corpus into a population guarantee.  It audits whether a comparison vocabulary preserves the public contract denominator.  Failed engine-pair stress, source sensitivity, and replay cost stay visible because the supported claim is finite; broader corpora or private behavior require a new admission step.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

Optimizer architectures supply the contract fields: System R separates access paths and costing~\cite{selinger1979access}; Volcano and Cascades expose rule search~\cite{graefe1990volcano,graefe1995cascades}; Calcite and Orca make adapters visible~\cite{begoli2018calcite,soliman2014orca}; and Garlic, BigDAWG, Snowflake, and Dremel surface placement and service stages~\cite{carey1995garlic,stonebraker2015bigdawg,dageville2016snowflake,melnik2010dremel}.  Benchmarks and testing systems standardize workloads, estimates, SQL equivalence, or bug finding~\cite{tpch,tpcds,leis2015good,chronis2024cardbench,chu2017cosette,wang2024qed,rigger2020sqlancer,rigger2020norec}; they supply controls, not equality predicates for public optimizer-contract labels.

\section{Conclusion}
OptSem-C checks the public-contract denominator that must hold before optimizer labels can support latency, cardinality, or plan-quality comparisons.  Within that finite boundary, coarse labels lose query-processing distinctions, while retained semantic fields restore auditable comparison.
