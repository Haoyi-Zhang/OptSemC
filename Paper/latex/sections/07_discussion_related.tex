\section{Discussion and Limitations}
\label{sec:discussion}

\textbf{Optimizer interfaces as semantic interfaces.}  Public optimizer claims should name action, guard, layer, placement, decision time, and evidence surface.  These fields keep adapters, remote sources, and service stages from collapsing into one phenomenon, so the reported difference is about the same public behavior.

\textbf{What changes in an evaluation.}  Before ranking latency, cardinality error, or plan quality, a study can test whether compared systems share the same public contract relation.  If not, it should retain separating fields, narrow the claim, or report mixed phenomena.  Tables~\ref{tab:anti-overfit-audit} and~\ref{tab:resource-profile} make this check rerunnable: repair evidence is frozen before counting, and replay cost is small compared with a full system-performance campaign.

\textbf{Protocol, scope, and refresh.}  JOB, TPC-H, and TPC-DS standardize inputs and metrics, not contract equality.  Every optimizer comparison still chooses a vocabulary, validates the denominator, computes the kernel, and reports retained fields.  Counts remain lower bounds over public evidence.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

System R separates access paths and costing~\cite{selinger1979access}; Volcano and Cascades expose rule search as an optimizer object~\cite{graefe1990volcano,graefe1995cascades}; Calcite and Orca make adapters and extensible rules part of the optimizer interface~\cite{begoli2018calcite,soliman2014orca}; and Garlic, BigDAWG, Snowflake, and Dremel make placement and service stages visible at the plan surface~\cite{carey1995garlic,stonebraker2015bigdawg,dageville2016snowflake,melnik2010dremel}.  Workload benchmarks such as TPC-H~\cite{tpch}, TPC-DS~\cite{tpcds}, JOB~\cite{leis2015good}, and CardBench~\cite{chronis2024cardbench} standardize inputs or estimates; Cosette~\cite{chu2017cosette}, QED~\cite{wang2024qed}, SQLancer~\cite{rigger2020sqlancer}, and NoREC~\cite{rigger2020norec} check semantics or wrong-answer behavior.  These lines supply objects and controls, but not equality for public optimizer-contract labels.

\section{Conclusion}
OptSem-C shows that denominator validity must precede cross-system optimizer ranking.  A public label such as \texttt{pushdown}, \texttt{join}, or \texttt{cost based} is useful only after the comparison preserves the fields that make the label comparable.  By fixing the schema, corpus, projection portfolio, repair universe, and replay checks before witness counting, OptSem-C turns that requirement into a finite audit: certify the public contract denominator first, then interpret latency, cardinality, or plan-quality differences.
