\section{Discussion and Limitations}
\label{sec:discussion}

\textbf{Protocol and use.}  Public optimizer claims should name action, guard, layer, placement, decision time, and evidence surface before latency, cardinality, or plan-quality rankings are interpreted.  JOB, TPC-H, and TPC-DS standardize inputs and metrics, not contract equality; OptSem-C audits which separating fields a study preserved.

\textbf{Boundary of the claim.}  OptSem-C does not infer private cost models, rank workload performance, or turn a finite public corpus into a population guarantee.  Pair concentration, failed engine-pair stress, source sensitivity, and replay cost stay visible; broader corpora, proprietary workloads, future engine versions, and out-of-schema operators require new admission.  Admission itself is a source-inspection step rather than an automated runtime claim; the resource tables measure only post-admission projection replay and SQL-denominator checks, not full setup or fresh source acquisition.

\textbf{Validity and stress reading.}  A false pair is not an execution bug, and a zero row is not an accuracy theorem; both are equality statements under the admitted public-contract relation.  The strongest regions are those where probe, feature, engine, SQL, scaling, and source-identity stresses agree; transfer failures and source-sensitive rows remain visible.  Layer and placement are predeclared query-processing fields with finite-stress support, while source admission remains the human-inspection step for any new denominator.

\textbf{Source and figure audit.}  Source-removal rows are identity audits, not exchangeability evidence: a removed source can erase original witnesses or induce new survivor pairs.  We report retained/induced counts, keep four engine pairs visible, and regenerate quantitative plots from frozen CSVs.  Replay costs cover post-admission projection and SQL checks, not source discovery or admission.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

Classical and cloud optimizer architectures define the audited fields: access paths, rule search, adapters, placement, and service stages~\cite{selinger1979access,graefe1990volcano,graefe1995cascades,begoli2018calcite,soliman2014orca,carey1995garlic,stonebraker2015bigdawg,dageville2016snowflake,melnik2010dremel}.  TPC-H, TPC-DS, JOB, CardEst, CardBench, Neo, and Bao evaluate workload, cardinality, and learned-planning outcomes~\cite{tpch,tpcds,leis2015good,leis2018looking,han2021cardest,chronis2024cardbench,marcus2019neo,marcus2021bao}.  Cosette, QED, SQLancer, and NoREC test query semantics or DBMS bugs~\cite{chu2017cosette,wang2024qed,rigger2020sqlancer,rigger2020norec}; OptSem-C audits denominators first.

\noindent\textbf{Conclusion.} OptSem-C exposes unsafe labels, witnesses, repairs, and finite stresses before runtime evidence is read.
