\section{Discussion and Limitations}
\label{sec:discussion}

\textbf{Scope and protocol.}  Public optimizer claims should name action, guard, layer, placement, decision time, and evidence surface; otherwise adapters, remote sources, and service stages collapse into one phenomenon.  Before ranking latency, cardinality error, or plan quality, a study should test whether compared systems share the same public contract relation, then retain separating fields, narrow the claim, or report mixed phenomena.  JOB, TPC-H, and TPC-DS standardize inputs and metrics, not contract equality; our counts remain lower bounds over public evidence.

\textbf{Boundary of the claim.}  OptSem-C does not infer private cost models, rank workload performance, or turn a finite public corpus into a population guarantee.  It audits whether a comparison vocabulary preserves the public contract denominator.  Failed engine-pair stress, source sensitivity, and replay cost remain visible because the supported claim is finite; broader corpora or private behavior require a new admission step.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

System R separates access paths and costing~\cite{selinger1979access}; Volcano and Cascades expose rule search~\cite{graefe1990volcano,graefe1995cascades}; Calcite and Orca make adapters and rules visible~\cite{begoli2018calcite,soliman2014orca}; and Garlic, BigDAWG, Snowflake, and Dremel surface placement and service stages~\cite{carey1995garlic,stonebraker2015bigdawg,dageville2016snowflake,melnik2010dremel}.  TPC-H~\cite{tpch}, TPC-DS~\cite{tpcds}, JOB~\cite{leis2015good}, and CardBench~\cite{chronis2024cardbench} standardize inputs or estimates, while Cosette~\cite{chu2017cosette}, QED~\cite{wang2024qed}, SQLancer~\cite{rigger2020sqlancer}, and NoREC~\cite{rigger2020norec} check equivalence or wrong answers.  These lines supply objects and controls, but not equality for public optimizer-contract labels.

\section{Conclusion}
OptSem-C treats optimizer-comparison labels as denominators, not harmless names.  Its finite audit checks whether public contracts remain comparable before latency, cardinality, or plan-quality results are interpreted.
