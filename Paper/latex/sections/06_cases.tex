Table~\ref{tab:cases} gives the concrete rule pairs behind the aggregate measurements.  The rows span the major mechanism families in Table~\ref{tab:mechanism-hotspots}, not unusually easy repairs.  C1 expands a remote-source aggregate probe with grouping and regional filtering.  BIGQUERY-FEDERATED contributes source-delegated filtering plus a CloudSQL blocking condition; TRINO-PUSH contributes aggregate pushdown, aggregate-expression blocking, and join-pushdown contracts.  Keyword projection sees ``pushdown'' in both maps, so $S_\pi(M_{\mathrm{BQ}})=S_\pi(M_{\mathrm{Trino}})$, but exact signatures separate filter delegation with stage evidence from aggregate delegation plus aggregate-expression denial.  The certificate checks exact inequality, projection equality, separation after retaining placement and operator/action variant, and a smaller-field counter-witness.

C2 separates PostgreSQL compile-time estimates~\cite{postgresql_docs} from BigQuery stages, shuffle metrics, and profile-time evidence~\cite{bigquery_docs}, and from Snowflake logical-plan evidence~\cite{snowflake_docs}.  The protected decision is whether an engine-selection report may compare estimates, service-stage graphs, and profiles as one evidence layer.  C3 separates compile-time choices from runtime adaptation: BigQuery repartition~\cite{bigquery_docs}, Spark AQE~\cite{spark_docs}, and ClickHouse automatic join conversion~\cite{clickhouse_docs} differ from static join selection, so adaptation claims must retain layer and decision time.  C4 contrasts DuckDB inline, materialize, and reuse guards~\cite{duckdb_docs} with PostgreSQL common-table-expression controls~\cite{postgresql_docs}; the repair changes a migration checklist from feature parity to guard-dependent obligations.  These cases are representative: the same rule schema and finite certificate checker cover all 498 headline witnesses.
