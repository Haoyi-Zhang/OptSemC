\section{Evaluation}
\label{sec:eval}
The evaluation follows the objection path a comparison paper would face.  Do coarse public vocabularies create denominator errors?  Are counts grounded by witnesses rather than names?  Do source, probe, feature, and engine stresses preserve a finite kernel without becoming population claims?  Are projection-audit stages cheap enough to rerun when the denominator changes?  The schema, projections, source manifest, and repair-field universe are frozen before counting.  Execution is a post-freeze SQL-denominator consistency check: it validates probes but never admits rules or chooses repair fields; a non-interference check verifies that DuckDB/PostgreSQL validation files are not consumed by collision or repair tables.  The table column ``false'' is contract-level: a declared vocabulary collapses unequal public optimizer-contract signatures before any latency, plan-quality, or cost claim is interpreted.

The comparison surfaces are executable projection vocabularies and controls, not DBMS bug finders such as SQLancer, NoREC, or SQLsmith; those tools test execution behavior under generated oracles, while OptSem-C audits whether a public comparison vocabulary has already collapsed unequal contracts.  Across the 26 public source surfaces, keyword, yes/no, and operator projections mirror feature labels, enablement controls, and operator-family labels; none exposes the complete reference contract payload.  Reference comparison is the negative control, one-field projections are diagnostic ablations, and strengthened projections restore fields to localize missing information.  All projections are fixed before witness counting and evaluated over the same source-linked maps.  The yes/no surface is the binary projection induced by public enablement or control claims; it is intentionally narrow because such controls often omit placement and evidence layers.  The question is how many witnesses are lost if equality is decided at that vocabulary, not whether every cited study made that exact projection.  Workload controls follow TPC-style analytical benchmarks, join-order diagnostics follow JOB~\cite{leis2015good} and its retrospective analysis~\cite{leis2018looking}, and the plan-quality control follows exact-cardinality testing~\cite{chaudhuri2009exact}.

\textbf{Coarse comparison induces repairable collisions.} The main empirical result is a finite counterexample to common optimizer-comparison vocabularies.  Table~\ref{tab:contract-collision} keeps public vocabularies, controls, stress rows, and repairs on the same scale: keyword declares 2,284 equivalences and collapses 254 unequal reference signatures, while operator-only declares 2,268 and collapses 238.  These errors are conditional on the vocabulary declaring equality over the fixed denominator.  They are also concentrated rather than uniform: the 498 headline records occur in four observed engine pairs and 486 distinct probes, with BigQuery--ClickHouse, BigQuery--Trino, ClickHouse--Trino, and Spark SQL--Trino carrying the visible hotspots.  This concentration is part of the claim, not an inconvenience hidden by the aggregate count.  Headline collisions have reference-signature Jaccard distance 1.0 and differ by 9.09 and 5.84 evidence atoms on average.  The strict reference and strengthened surface controls have zero false pairs; one-field stress rows show that a single optimizer dimension can make the denominator worse rather than safer.

Finite resampling keeps keyword at roughly 10--12\% and operator labels at roughly 9--12\%.  Single-source removal is stricter to read: removing one source can delete original witnesses and create new ones, so the audit reports both nonzero counts and witness-identity movement rather than treating leave-one-source as population sampling.  The repair column localizes the missing semantics: placement repairs keyword, layer repairs the operator row, and the strengthened semantic surface returns to zero false pairs.  The sparse yes/no row remains a narrow witness family.

\textbf{Projection kernels are measurable objects.} Figure~\ref{fig:projection-information} reports the finite information profile of representative projections.  Reference signatures form 1,288 observed classes.  Keyword projection compresses them into 148 projected classes, 81 of which contain multiple reference classes, retaining 64\% of empirical signature entropy and inflating declared equivalence by 12.5\%.  Operator-only projection retains more entropy but still leaves 191 colliding projected classes and 238 pairwise collisions.  Placement-only and state-only surfaces demonstrate the opposite failure mode: a single field creates very large kernels.  The strengthened surface projection still has projected collisions at the signature-class level, but it does not produce any pairwise collision over the engine-probe comparison set.  Thus the kernel is finite, populated, and repairable in the observed comparison relation.

\textbf{Resolution-lattice stress test.}  Table~\ref{tab:resolution-lattice} asks which retained-field vocabularies are safe when a comparison may keep any subset of public optimizer fields.  The exhaustive test enumerates all 256 subsets over the same 88,536 engine--probe comparisons.  This is not a repeated-significance search: every unsafe cell is a concrete counter-witness in a fixed finite relation, so no multiple-testing correction is being used to select a result.  The empty vocabulary declares 59,546 unsupported equivalences, and every singleton semantic field remains unsafe: operator leaves 238 collisions, kind 252, layer 449, and placement 10,702.  Excluding action-variant identifiers, the semantic lattice has 128 subsets, 44 unsafe regions, minimum safe field count two, 14 minimal safe vocabularies, and eight maximal unsafe vocabularies.  Ten concrete engine--probe witnesses cover all unsafe retained-field regions, so the monotone frontier certificate is finite rather than anecdotal.

The robustness view in Table~\ref{tab:robustness} checks that the headline result is not an effect of a single denominator.  The micro rate conditions on the projection declaring equivalence; finite bands and probe-cluster resampling summarize dispersion over declared equivalences and probe clusters, not population inference; the pair-macro rate averages within engine pairs; and the leave-one-source range removes all rules supported by one public source.  Keyword and operator-only collisions remain nonzero across these views, with leave-one-source conditional-rate ranges $[0.017,0.144]$ and $[0.032,0.148]$ respectively; the Boundary panel records how much of the original witness set is retained and how many new witnesses are induced by deletion.

\input{tables/tab_robustness_replay_dashboard}

The evidence side is cross-checked without retraining on headline records: every collision has at least two public source records and side-balanced support.  The freeze manifest records the schema, feature domain, source manifest, admitted rules, projection portfolio, motif crosswalk, result-determining implementation, and replay entrypoints before witness counting; changing them requires a full replay.

The Boundary panel in Table~\ref{tab:robustness} is a ledger rather than a victory table.  Keyword and operator-only remain nonzero after every leave-one-source deletion, but keyword retains as little as 2\% of its original witness identities in the most disruptive deletion and can induce many new witnesses.  The point is therefore not that source removal preserves the same sample; it is that a nonzero kernel remains while the identity movement is exposed.  Their 10\% probe subsamples remain nonzero in all trials, so the kernel is not produced by one source or probe slice inside the frozen denominator.  The yes/no projection is source-sensitive and stays out of the broad robustness claim.  The reported layer-and-placement basis resolves feature-family and engine-family stress, while point-learned engine-pair repairs fail on held-out pairs; that failure keeps the claim finite-denominator rather than learned-transfer.

Because the 287-rule corpus is finite, the remaining checks test whether the kernel is a one-source or one-shape accident.  The seven-engine denominator contributes admission evidence, controls, and source-removal stress; the 498 headline false-witness records occupy four observed engine pairs and 486 distinct probes.  They touch all 12 feature dimensions and fully cover the value domain for 10 of them, while every probe carries at most one headline witness.  The dispersion matters because the result is concentrated in interpretable engine pairs without collapsing to a single probe or feature value.

\textbf{Finite-audit replay cost.}  A VLDB evaluation should not have to trust an expensive hand audit for the comparison kernel.  The Replay panel in Table~\ref{tab:robustness} reports projection audit, SQL validation, paper-facing replay total, and an 8$\times$ lift over the same constructed relation.  The timing rows are single validation-host deterministic replay bounds for a frozen relation, not stochastic performance comparisons; they exclude setup, paper build, package construction, source acquisition, and fresh DBMS reruns.  The same panel adds projection-level dispersion: full audits cover 88,536 engine--probe checks per projection above 50,000 checks/s, the 8$\times$ lift repeats the relation to 708,288 checks per projection above 190,000 checks/s with prefix-scaling $R^2\geq0.98$, streaming maintenance has zero drift, and the largest source refresh affects 46 rules, 4,216 maps, and 14.3\% of rule--probe rows.

\textbf{Repair certificates identify the missing semantics.} Table~\ref{tab:contract-collision} reports collision counts, severity, and minimum repair diagnosis.  The certificate evidence is stricter than a post-hoc label choice: each certificate must match the tested projection, differ under reference contracts, separate under the proposed repair, and survive counter-witness checks against smaller universal repairs.  Placement repairs keyword collisions; layer repairs operator-only collisions; layer plus placement repairs all 498 headline records without action-variant labels.  Residual paths show why one field is insufficient: operator nearly repairs keyword but leaves six collisions, and placement alone still fails for the operator row.  Fold-local repairs can underfit the global denominator; layer+placement resolves every probe-fold collision.  Figure~\ref{fig:semantic-frontier} gives the scoped repair frontier: safe subsets repair in-scope collisions, unsafe subsets retain counter-witnesses, and layer+placement is a headline-witness basis rather than a globally safe vocabulary.

\textbf{Collisions have mechanisms and hotspots.}  The repair fields are query-processing boundaries: placement separates local execution, source delegation, distributed workers, and cloud services; layer and decision time separate rewrite, planning, distribution, and runtime adaptation; operator and observability keep estimates, plans, and profiles distinct.  The finite witness ledger attributes all 498 headline records to action variants, 466 to placement, 430 to optimizer layer, 366 to plan evidence, 362 to decision time, and 281 to operator family.  The hotspots are also interpretable: keyword collisions are concentrated in BigQuery--ClickHouse and BigQuery--Trino comparisons, while operator collisions concentrate in ClickHouse--Trino and Spark SQL--Trino.

\textbf{Workload sensitivity.} Controlled feature-toggle movements over CTE reuse, source boundary, statistics need, distribution, and adaptivity show that comparability is a relation between engine contracts and query structure.  The largest movements are finite neighborhoods over the frozen feature space rather than broad performance claims: DuckDB reuse structure has the largest mean signature movement, Trino statistics need has broad nonzero support, and distribution/adaptivity toggles move different engines through different public-contract coordinates.
