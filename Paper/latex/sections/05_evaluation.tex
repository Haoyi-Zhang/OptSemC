\section{Evaluation}
\label{sec:eval}
The evaluation follows the comparison decision path rather than the execution order.  First, it asks whether public comparison vocabularies create denominator errors.  Second, it grounds the aggregate result with concrete witnesses.  Third, it checks whether the result survives source, probe, feature, and engine stress without turning boundary cases into claims.  Fourth, it reports the runtime and resource cost of rerunning the finite audit.  The schema, projections, source manifest, and repair-field universe are frozen before derived tables are generated; scripts then rebuild the reference signatures, projected equivalence classes, repair certificates, robustness tables, and paper tables from those inputs.  This protocol is intentionally stricter than selecting a successful run after inspection: execution validates SQL probes, but it never admits rules, edits the projection portfolio, or chooses repair fields.  The table column ``false'' is contract-level: it means that a declared vocabulary collapses unequal public optimizer-contract signatures.  Such a collision makes the comparison denominator semantically invalid before any latency, plan-quality, or cost claim is interpreted.

The baselines are executable comparison vocabularies, not DBMS-testing tools.  Cosette~\cite{chu2017cosette} and QED~\cite{wang2024qed} verify SQL equivalence.  Stochastic SQL testing~\cite{slutz1998massive}, SQLsmith~\cite{sqlsmith}, SQLancer/PQS~\cite{rigger2020sqlancer}, and NoREC~\cite{rigger2020norec} target DBMS bugs or wrong answers.  Neither family defines equality for public optimizer-contract denominators: two systems can return the same SQL result and still expose different public contracts for where optimization occurs, when a decision is made, and what plan evidence is observable.  To keep the projection portfolio practice-aligned, we audit the 26 public source surfaces used by the corpus: 26 expose keyword or feature-name labels, 12 expose enablement or yes/no controls, 26 expose operator-family labels, and none exposes the complete reference contract payload used by OptSem-C.  Keyword, yes/no, and operator projections mirror public comparison surfaces; reference comparison is the negative control; one-field projections test whether a single optimizer dimension can stand in for contract semantics; strengthened projections restore fields to localize missing information.  All projections are fixed before witness counting and evaluated over the same source-linked maps.  The question is how many witnesses are lost if equality is decided at that vocabulary, not whether every cited study made that exact projection.  Feature-name labels come from Trino pushdown documentation~\cite{trino_pushdown_docs} and Presto documentation~\cite{presto_docs}; workload labels use TPC-H~\cite{tpch} and TPC-DS~\cite{tpcds}; plan-quality controls follow JOB~\cite{leis2015good}, later JOB diagnostics~\cite{leis2018looking}, and exact-cardinality testing~\cite{chaudhuri2009exact}.

\textbf{Coarse comparison induces repairable collisions.} The main empirical result is a finite counterexample to common optimizer-comparison vocabularies: keyword and operator-only views both declare hundreds of equivalences that disagree under reference public-contract signatures.  Table~\ref{tab:contract-collision} marks these two public vocabularies as the headline rows while keeping controls, stress rows, and repairs on the same scale.  Keyword declares 2,284 equivalences and collapses 254 unequal reference signatures; operator-only declares 2,268 equivalences and collapses 238.  These errors are conditional on the vocabulary declaring equality, which is the exact denominator a user inherits from a feature matrix.  The witnesses are not near duplicates: headline collisions have reference-signature Jaccard distance 1.0 and differ by 9.09 and 5.84 evidence atoms on average.  The strict reference and strengthened surface controls both have zero false pairs, while one-field stress rows such as placement-only and state-only show that a single optimizer dimension can make the denominator much worse rather than safer.

The result is not a sampling accident or a synonym-list accident.  Wilson and probe-cluster bootstrap intervals keep keyword at roughly 10--12\% and operator-only at roughly 9--12\%, and the collision count remains nonzero for both projections after removing any single public source.  The repair column localizes the missing semantics instead of merely rejecting abstraction: placement repairs every keyword collision, layer repairs every operator-only collision, and the strengthened semantic-surface vocabulary returns to the reference denominator with zero projection-induced false pairs.  The sparse yes/no row is treated as a real but narrow witness family rather than as the headline robustness result.

\textbf{Projection kernels are measurable objects.} Figure~\ref{fig:projection-information} reports the finite information profile of representative projections.  Reference signatures form 1,288 observed classes.  Keyword projection compresses them into 148 projected classes, 81 of which contain multiple reference classes, retaining 64\% of empirical signature entropy and inflating declared equivalence by 12.5\%.  Operator-only projection retains more entropy but still leaves 191 colliding projected classes and 238 pairwise collisions.  Placement-only and state-only baselines demonstrate the opposite failure mode: a single field creates very large kernels.  The strengthened surface projection still has projected collisions at the signature-class level, but it does not produce any pairwise collision over the engine-probe comparison set.  Thus the kernel is finite, populated, and repairable in the observed comparison relation.

\textbf{Resolution-lattice stress test.}  Table~\ref{tab:resolution-lattice} asks which retained-field vocabularies are safe when a comparison may keep any subset of public optimizer fields.  The exhaustive test enumerates all 256 subsets over the same 88,536 engine--probe comparisons.  This is not a repeated-significance search: every unsafe cell is a concrete counter-witness in a fixed finite relation, so no multiple-testing correction is being used to select a result.  The empty vocabulary declares 59,546 unsupported equivalences, and every singleton semantic field remains unsafe: operator leaves 238 collisions, kind 252, layer 449, and placement 10,702.  Excluding action-variant identifiers, the semantic lattice has 128 subsets, 44 unsafe regions, minimum safe field count two, 14 minimal safe vocabularies, and eight maximal unsafe vocabularies.  Ten concrete engine--probe witnesses cover all unsafe retained-field regions, so the monotone frontier certificate is finite rather than anecdotal.

The robustness view in Table~\ref{tab:robustness} checks that the headline result is not an effect of a single denominator.  The micro rate conditions on the baseline declaring equivalence; Wilson and probe-cluster bootstrap intervals quantify uncertainty over declared equivalences and probe clusters; the pair-macro rate averages within engine pairs; and the leave-one-source range removes all rules supported by one public source.  Keyword and operator-only collisions remain visible across these views, with leave-one-source conditional-rate ranges $[0.017,0.144]$ and $[0.032,0.148]$ respectively.

\input{tables/tab_robustness}

The evidence side is cross-checked without retraining on the headline records.  Every collision has at least two public source records and side-balanced support.  The freeze manifest records the schema, feature domain, source manifest, admitted rules, projection portfolio, motif crosswalk, result-determining projection code, compute/check scripts, and replay entrypoints before witness counting; changing those inputs requires a full replay rather than a table edit.  Table~\ref{tab:anti-overfit-audit} is therefore read as a boundary ledger, not as a victory table.  Keyword and operator-only survive every leave-one-source removal, and their 10\% probe subsamples remain nonzero in all trials, but these are stress checks inside the frozen denominator rather than independent-population tests.  The yes/no projection is source-sensitive and is not used as a broad robustness claim.  The fixed layer-and-placement basis resolves feature-family and engine-family stress, but point-learned engine-pair repairs fail on held-out pairs; we report that failure because it blocks a stronger learned-transfer claim.  The supported claim is finite-denominator: coarse vocabularies collapse source-supported signatures inside the frozen relation, and the reported basis is a fixed semantic basis from the predeclared field universe checked against stress splits rather than a repair learned from outcome behavior.

\textbf{Finite-audit replay cost.}  A VLDB evaluation should not have to trust an expensive hand audit.  Table~\ref{tab:resource-profile} reports the cloud replay cost for projection audit, fixed-basis repair, SQL validation, and an 8$\times$ deterministic lift of the finite comparison relation.  These rows are selected finite-audit stages, not a cold-start artifact build, archive rebuild, or paper-regeneration wall clock.  The lift is not a new corpus, engine-count, source-count, or semantic-domain claim; it checks the comparison inner loop after the denominator has already been constructed.  Table~\ref{tab:scalability-family} adds projection-level dispersion: full audits cover all 88,536 engine--probe checks per projection above 50,000 checks/s, the 8$\times$ lift reaches 708,288 checks per projection (2,833,152 aggregate input rows across four projections) above 200,000 checks/s with prefix-scaling $R^2\geq0.98$, streaming maintenance has zero drift, and the largest source refresh affects 46 rules, 4,216 maps, and 14.3\% of rule--probe rows.

\textbf{Repair certificates identify the missing semantics.} Table~\ref{tab:contract-collision} reports collision counts, severity, and minimum repair diagnosis.  Table~\ref{tab:repair-certificate-summary} breaks the repair evidence into residual paths and probe-fold stability checks: placement repairs keyword collisions, layer repairs operator-only collisions, and one fixed layer-and-placement basis repairs all 498 keyword, yes/no, and operator-only records without fine-grained action-variant labels.  Each certificate is a finite proof obligation: every counted record differs under reference contracts while matching the tested projection, is separated by the reported repair, and has a grounded counter-witness against smaller universal repairs.

\input{tables/tab_repair_certificate_summary}

The residuals show that operator nearly repairs keyword but leaves six collisions, placement eliminates keyword loss, and layer repairs operator-only where placement alone leaves residual errors.  The fold-local column checks anti-overfitting from the opposite direction: a repair chosen within one probe fold can underfit the global denominator, but the fixed layer and placement basis resolves every probe-fold collision.  Figure~\ref{fig:semantic-frontier} summarizes the repair frontier: safe retained-field subsets repair every in-scope collision, unsafe subsets retain grounded counter-witnesses, and layer+placement is one interpretable fixed two-field basis among the minimum safe sets for the 498 headline records.

\input{figures/fig_semantic_frontier_plot}

The attribution and interpretation columns explain why the repair fields are query-processing fields: placement separates local execution, source delegation, distributed workers, and cloud-service execution; layer and decision time separate rewrite, planning, distribution, and runtime adaptation; operator and observability keep estimates, plans, and profiles from being interchangeable.  The highest-attribution fields are also the fields that repair the collisions.

\textbf{Collisions have mechanisms and hotspots.} Table~\ref{tab:mechanism-hotspots} groups witnesses into optimizer mechanisms and engine-pair hotspots.  The dominant mechanisms are action variant, placement, layer, plan evidence, and decision time: exactly the boundaries between source delegation and local planning, runtime adaptivity and compile-time planning, and stage graphs and local estimates.
